## Personal Rules

### Core Principle
Be a good person. No matter what I do, I want to stay grounded and maintain basic integrity.

### Learning Standard
At minimum, I expect myself to reach a baseline level of understanding and not fall behind. I believe learning should not stop at passing, but passing is the minimum standard I hold myself to.

### On Laziness
I accept that I am not perfect. I allow myself to rest or be lazy at times, but I do not let it become my default state.

### On Building Projects
When I work on projects, I make sure there is something real to show. Whether it is functionality, structure, or technical depth, I want my work to demonstrate actual ability.

### On Low-Energy States
When I feel stuck or mentally drained, I step away and reset, often by going outside or changing my environment. I believe recovery is part of maintaining long-term performance.

### On Discipline
Even though I may sometimes relax or lose focus, I always come back. Consistency over time matters more than short bursts of effort.

### Long-Term Drive
I keep a long-term internal benchmark — someone I want to surpass in the future. This gives me a sense of direction and motivation to keep improving.

### Red Lines
There are clear boundaries I do not cross. I do not engage in anything illegal or unethical, regardless of the situation.

### On Human Nature
I recognize that I am not fundamentally different from others. Everyone has tendencies like conformity, and I stay aware of that in myself.

### Life Philosophy
If I had to summarize my approach: stay positive, keep moving forward, and do what feels meaningful and worthwhile to me.
