## Mistakes and Growth

### Early Missteps
One of the biggest mistakes I made was not taking my studies seriously during high school. This decision affected my academic path later on and caused me to lose valuable time during my early college years.

### Period of Uncertainty
Between 2019 and 2021, after leaving college and during the pandemic, I went through a period of confusion and uncertainty. I struggled with direction, self-assessment, and understanding what I truly wanted to pursue. This was a challenging time where I lacked both clarity and structure.

### Root Causes
Looking back, my main issues were a lack of discipline and insufficient information about how to plan my path effectively. I did not have a clear understanding of how to approach my future, and I was not consistent enough in executing my goals.

### Turning Point
Around 2020, I experienced a turning point where I realized I needed to take control of my life. Seeing the gap between myself and my peers made me reflect seriously on my situation and motivated me to start making changes.

### Growth and Progress
After that period, I began to improve steadily. While I still sometimes struggle with overthinking, I have developed a much stronger sense of direction and a better ability to push myself forward. My progress since then has been much faster compared to before.

### Perspective on Comparison
In the past, I often compared myself to others and felt anxious about my position. Over time, I have shifted my mindset and now focus more on long-term growth. I believe that with consistent effort, I can reach a level that surpasses most people over time.

### Current Limitation
At this stage, my biggest gap is still a lack of industry experience. However, I see this not as a permanent weakness, but as a natural next step in my development.

### Reflection
If I could go back, I would tell my younger self to take responsibility earlier and stay disciplined. However, I also believe that those experiences were necessary, as they shaped my current mindset.

### Core Lesson
The most important lesson I have learned is to trust myself, stay consistent, and focus on doing my best without leaving regrets. Progress comes from sustained effort over time.
