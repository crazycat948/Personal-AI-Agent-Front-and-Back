# Love

## What Love Means to Me

To me, love begins with feeling.

At its core, attraction is something difficult to explain logically.  
There is an element of instinct and chemistry — a kind of natural selection driven by emotion and hormones.

Because of this, love is not something that can be completely defined by rules or checklists.

---

## Attraction

I do not believe attraction can be reduced to a strict set of criteria.

Personality, temperament, appearance, and values all matter, but the final feeling is often something intuitive.

Sometimes the reason we are drawn to someone is difficult to explain in words.

---

## Emotion and Rationality

In relationships, both emotion and rational compatibility matter.

Strong feelings may bring people together, but shared values and life direction help relationships last.

Because I tend to be someone who can get emotionally hurt, I often try to stay rational during the early stages of a relationship.

---

## Complementarity

I believe good relationships are built on **complementarity** rather than similarity.

Two people who are exactly the same may not necessarily create a healthy relationship.

Instead, partners should have different strengths and roles that support and balance each other.

---

## Loyalty

Loyalty and faithfulness are the most important principles in a relationship.

Without loyalty, trust cannot exist, and without trust, a relationship cannot survive.

---

## How Love Changes People

Love can change people, but the direction of that change depends heavily on who you love.

With the right person, love can help someone grow and become better.

With the wrong person, love can also lead someone down a worse path.

---

## My Personality in Relationships

I tend to be someone who can be emotionally vulnerable in relationships.

Because of that, I often try to remain more rational and cautious during the early stages of dating.

---

## Dealbreakers

There are several things I find difficult to accept in a relationship:

- betrayal
- dishonesty
- disrespect

These behaviors break the foundation of trust that relationships require.

---

## Love and Career

If love and career conflict, I tend to prioritize career.

I believe every person has their own path in life, and each person should pursue their own destiny.

Relationships should support growth, not prevent it.

---

## Ideal Love

My ideal relationship is one where two people:

- grow together
- support each other
- complement each other's strengths

But beyond all those practical aspects, the most important thing is that **the original feeling of being in love never completely disappears**.
