# Hobbies

## Fitness

I regularly go to the gym.

This habit actually started during a difficult emotional period in my life.  
At first, training was simply a way to release frustration, but over time it turned into a stable part of my routine.

Fitness eventually became something deeper than just exercise.  
It represents discipline, emotional balance, and a way to reset my mind.

Among sports, I prefer **individual competitive sports**, such as tennis.  
I enjoy the clarity of one-on-one competition where the outcome depends purely on the two players involved.

Swimming is something I learned as a child because my parents insisted on it, but it was never something I personally loved.

## Gaming

I enjoy games that are easy to get into and relaxing to play.

Competitive games I’ve spent time on include:

- League of Legends
- Overwatch

At the same time, I also enjoy slower and more relaxing experiences such as:

- JRPGs
- farming / simulation games
- casual single-player games

Games for me are less about intense competition and more about immersion and enjoyment.

## Anime and Manga

Anime and manga are some of my biggest interests.

Some of my favorite series include:

- Clannad  
- Your Lie in April  
- Neon Genesis Evangelion  
- Violet Evergarden  

These works stand out to me because of their emotional depth and the way they explore human relationships.

Recently, my favorite anime-related film has been the **Chainsaw Man Reze Arc**.

I’m also interested in drawing and manga creation, especially short comic formats like **four-panel comics**.

## Basketball

I’m an NBA fan.

For many years I supported the **Dallas Mavericks**.  
When Luka Dončić moved to the **Los Angeles Lakers**, I started watching more Lakers games.

However, I wouldn’t call myself a true Lakers fan because I’ve never been particularly fond of LeBron.

At the moment, the team I feel closest to is probably the **San Antonio Spurs**, partly because they represent Texas.

Interestingly, even though I live in Houston, I’ve never really connected with the Rockets — their playstyle always felt a bit too soft to me.

## Traveling and Exploring Cities

I enjoy traveling and exploring cities, especially in slow and relaxed ways.

My favorite travel moments are usually very simple:

- riding a bike through Central Park in New York  
- walking through quiet neighborhoods near Kerry Park in Seattle  
- watching the sunset on the beach in San Diego  

These quiet moments help me feel the real rhythm of a place.

## Observing People

One of my more unusual hobbies is observing people.

When interacting with others, I often form mental models of their personalities.  
Sometimes I can even imagine how they would respond in conversations — the tone they would use and the things they might say.

In a way, real-life social environments sometimes feel like watching characters in a story.

---

## My Ideal Weekend

My ideal weekend is quiet and simple.

In my imagination, I live near a small mountain close to the city.  
Early in the morning, I would climb the mountain alone and watch the city slowly wake up.

Feeling the cold morning air and the calm after physical movement is something I find deeply satisfying.

After returning home, I would take a short nap, eat a light lunch, and spend the afternoon slowly walking through the city.

In the evening, I would relax at home and play some games.

Simple moments like these feel peaceful and complete to me.
