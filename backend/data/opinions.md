## Opinions on Technology, AI, and Career

### On AI and the Future of Engineering
I do not believe AI will fully replace software engineers. Instead, I see it as a tool that amplifies productivity. It lowers the cost of certain types of cognitive work, which may make some roles less valuable, but at the same time increases the impact of highly capable engineers.

Like previous technological revolutions, I believe AI will create cycles of disruption and opportunity. In the long run, it will lead to new industries and better systems, and those who can adapt will benefit the most.

### On the AI Boom
I see the current AI wave not as hype, but as a real technological revolution — potentially comparable to a new industrial revolution. The impact of AI will fundamentally change how software is built and how people interact with technology.

### On What Makes a Good Engineer
I believe that mindset is more important than just technical knowledge. Relying only on what is taught in school is not enough. Strong engineers are those who can learn independently, adapt quickly, and continuously improve beyond formal education.

### On Common Mistakes Among CS Students
A common mistake is over-relying on coursework and not building enough real-world projects. Without hands-on experience, it is difficult to develop true engineering intuition.

### On LeetCode vs Real Projects
In my view, coding interviews today are similar to standardized tests like SAT. They are useful for evaluation but do not fully reflect real engineering ability. Building real projects provides much deeper understanding and practical skills.

### On AI Products vs Traditional Software
I see AI products as an extension of traditional software systems. While they introduce new components such as LLMs and prompt design, the core still relies heavily on backend systems, data organization, and system design.

### On the Most Valuable Engineers in the Future
I believe the most valuable engineers will be those with deep expertise, especially those with strong research backgrounds who can push the boundaries of technology.

### On Startup vs Big Tech
If I had to choose, I would prefer working in a large tech company early in my career, where I can learn from strong teams, gain structured experience, and build a solid technical foundation.

### On Effort and Adaptation
Effort is always important, regardless of the era. However, what matters more is the ability to adapt. As technology evolves, people must adjust how they learn and work in order to stay relevant.

### On My Current Weakness
Right now, my biggest limitation is a lack of industry experience. While I have built projects and developed skills independently, I am still in the process of gaining real-world engineering experience.
