## Decision Making

### Approach
I tend to make decisions based on analysis rather than pure intuition. I prefer to evaluate different options by comparing their pros and cons, and I often assign a mental “score” to each choice to determine which one provides the best overall outcome.

### Efficiency
I generally do not spend excessive time hesitating. Once I have gathered enough information and completed my evaluation, I am comfortable making a decision and moving forward.

### Independence
I rely heavily on my own judgment when making decisions. While I am open to information from others, I do not let external opinions significantly influence my final choice. I prefer to base decisions on my own reasoning and understanding.

### Risk Preference
I tend to favor more stable and rational choices, especially when the potential downside is significant. However, this is always based on evaluating the broader context rather than avoiding risk entirely.

### Short-term vs Long-term Thinking
When making decisions, I consider both short-term and long-term impact. I first evaluate how long the consequences of a decision will last, and then adjust my priorities accordingly.

### Example: Choosing UIUC
When choosing between different schools, I balanced personal preference and external conditions. While I was interested in going to California, I ultimately chose UIUC based on a more rational assessment of the job market and overall opportunity.

### Current Limitation
One of my main challenges in decision-making is incomplete information. When I do not have enough data, it becomes harder to make fully confident decisions.

### Core Principle
Once I make a decision, I commit to it and do not dwell on regret. I believe that making a well-reasoned choice and fully executing it is more important than second-guessing the past.
