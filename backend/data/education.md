## Education Path Summary

Yifan Ren completed his early education in Changsha, China before immigrating to the United States with his family.  
He attended high school in Texas and later began his university education in the United States.  

After initially attending the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, he transferred to Houston Community College due to personal and academic adjustment challenges. He later transferred to the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD) in January 2023 to complete his undergraduate studies in Computer Science.

He has been admitted to graduate programs in Computer Science at both the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD) and the University of California, Davis (UC Davis).

## Houston Community College (HCC)
Location: Houston, Texas, USA  
Study Period: 2020 – January 2023  

Experience:
After leaving the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, Yifan returned to Houston and spent several years studying at HCC.  
Although the academic pressure was relatively manageable, this period carried significant personal pressure.  

He did not form close friendships during this time and did not particularly enjoy the community college academic structure.  
However, this period became an important turning point where he reflected on his academic direction and worked toward transferring to a university again.

---

## University of Nebraska–Lincoln (UNL)
Location: Lincoln, Nebraska, USA  
Study Period: 2019 – 2020  

Experience:
This was Yifan's first time living in the northern United States and in a relatively small city.  
The cold climate and unfamiliar environment made the experience difficult to adapt to.  

Additionally, he felt that his academic preparation from high school had been insufficient, which made the coursework challenging.  
Due to these difficulties, he decided to leave UNL and later continue his studies elsewhere.

# High School

## Seven Lakes High School
Location: Katy, Texas, USA  
Graduation Year: 2018  

Experience:
Seven Lakes High School marked Yifan's first experience living and studying in the United States after immigrating with his family.

At the beginning, he struggled significantly with the English language and communication, which made the early period quite difficult.  
He became quieter and less socially active during this time.

Later in high school, he gradually improved his English and formed a few close friendships.  
However, he also feels that he did not take his academic work as seriously as he could have during this period.

Experience:
