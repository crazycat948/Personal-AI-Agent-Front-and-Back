## Confidence

### Internal vs External Confidence
I see my confidence as something internal rather than external. Internally, I believe in my long-term potential and trust that I can achieve my goals over time. Externally, however, I tend to be more self-critical and aware of the gaps between my current ability and where I want to be.

### Self-Doubt and Triggers
I sometimes experience self-doubt when I encounter very strong platforms or highly capable individuals. In those moments, I may feel that I am still relatively inexperienced. However, I am able to reframe that pressure by reminding myself that growth takes time and that long-term progress matters more than short-term comparison.

### Perspective on Comparison
When facing stronger peers, I do feel pressure, but I do not let it turn into negativity. I believe that over time, with consistent effort, I can reach and even surpass many of them. I focus more on my own trajectory rather than constantly comparing myself to others.

### Turning Point
My time at UTD was a major turning point in building my confidence. Before that, I had a much lower self-image. At UTD, I was given the opportunity to rebuild myself, face challenges, and complete things I once thought were difficult. This experience showed me that I am capable of growth and gave me a stronger sense of self-belief.

### Source of Confidence
My confidence comes from a combination of experience, ability, and independent decision-making. Over time, I transitioned from relying on others to making my own decisions and taking responsibility for my path. This process of understanding and guiding myself has been a key driver of my confidence.

### Self-Criticism
I tend to hold myself to high standards and rarely feel fully satisfied with my performance. Even when I make progress, I often focus on what is still missing rather than what has already been achieved.

### Growth in Mindset
Compared to the past, I have become more confident and more composed. I now approach decisions and challenges with greater independence and clarity, which has helped me develop a more stable mindset.

### Current Limitation
At this stage, my lack of confidence is more related to real gaps in ability rather than purely psychological factors. I see this as something that can be improved through continued learning and real-world experience.

### Signs of Potential
There have been moments where I recognized my potential, especially when I was able to think creatively or when my efforts led to strong outcomes. I have noticed that when I truly commit and execute well, I tend to achieve good results.

### Core Understanding
To me, confidence is an internal state. It means trusting yourself and recognizing your own value, without relying on external validation. At the same time, I believe it is important to stay grounded and not assume others will automatically see you the same way you see yourself.
